Tomato_Bacterial_spot
Description: Bacterial spot disease caused by Xanthomonas bacteria, appearing as dark spots on leaves, reducing photosynthetic ability.
Solution:
-Use disease-resistant varieties.
-Apply copper-based fungicides.
-Avoid overhead watering to minimize spread.

Tomato_Early_blight
Description: Early blight caused by the fungus Alternaria solani, forming concentric ring spots on leaves and causing early leaf drop.
Solution:
-Rotate crops, avoid continuous planting of tomatoes in the same soil.
-Use fungicides containing Mancozeb.
-Ensure good air circulation around plants.

Tomato_healthy
Description: Healthy tomato plants, free from diseases.
Solution:
-Apply balanced fertilizers to boost resistance.
-Water appropriately, avoiding excess.
-Regularly inspect plants to detect diseases early.

Tomato_Late_blight
Description: Late blight caused by the fungus Phytophthora infestans, leading to rotting of leaves, stems, and fruits.
Solution:
-Apply copper-based fungicides or Chlorothalonil.
-Remove infected leaves to prevent spread.
-Use disease-resistant varieties.

Tomato_Leaf_Mold
Description: Leaf mold disease caused by the fungus Passalora fulva, forming yellow or brown mold on the underside of leaves.
Solution:
-Improve ventilation in greenhouses or gardens.
-Apply fungicides containing Chlorothalonil or Difenoconazole.
-Avoid overhead watering.

Tomato_Septoria_leaf_spot
Description: Septoria leaf spot caused by the fungus Septoria lycopersici, forming small dark brown spots on leaves.
Solution:
-Remove infected leaves.
-Apply fungicides containing Mancozeb or Copper.
-Space plants adequately to improve ventilation.

Tomato_Spider_mites_Two_spotted_spider_mite
Description: Caused by two-spotted spider mites (Tetranychus urticae), leading to yellowing and weakening of leaves.
Solution:
-Spray Neem oil to control mites.
-Use beneficial predators like ladybugs to eliminate mites.
-Increase humidity to reduce mite development.

Tomato__Target_Spot
Description: Target spot caused by the fungus Corynespora cassiicola, forming large spots with gray centers.
Solution:
-Use fungicides containing Azoxystrobin or Difenoconazole.
-Improve air circulation around plants.
-Rotate crops.

Tomato__Tomato_mosaic_virus
Description: Mosaic disease caused by Tomato Mosaic Virus (TMV), leading to leaf deformation and reduced yield.
Solution:
-Use virus-resistant varieties.
-Regularly disinfect gardening tools.
-Control insects like thrips, which spread the virus.

Tomato__Tomato_YellowLeaf__Curl_Virus
Description: Yellow leaf curl disease caused by Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV), causing leaf curling, yellowing, and stunted growth.
Solution:
-Use virus-resistant varieties.
-Prevent whiteflies (disease vectors) with protective netting.
-Spray Neem oil to control insects.
